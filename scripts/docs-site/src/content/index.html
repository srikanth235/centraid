Centraid · the manual

Your data. Your apps. Your devices.

Centraid is personal software with the ownership inverted. One vault on your own machine holds the canonical record of your life on a single shared ontology — no silos. Apps are thin projections over it, built and rebuilt by an agent to fit you. Devices are clients of one gateway you run, connected peer-to-peer. Everything in this manual branches from those three pieces.

The deep end

The ontology has its own chapter.

The claim that Centraid has no data silos rests on one artifact: a single person-owned data model where identity, time, money, content, activity, observation and relationships are declared exactly once, and every app either extends or references them — never re-declares them. It is rendered as a live, explorable document: the full schema, the entity map, the ownership matrix, the gateway contract, and the ten rules that keep it honest.

Eleven schemas · one spine · consent checked on every read and write. Read the ontology →
