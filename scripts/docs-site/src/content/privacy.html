Privacy policy · effective 23 July 2026

Your gateway keeps the keys.

Centraid is local-first software. Your gateway stores your vault and connector credentials. The optional Centraid Assist ceremony helps your gateway obtain Google authorization; it is not a hosted Google-data or token store.

This policy explains how Centraid accesses, uses, stores, shares, retains, and deletes Google user data. It applies to the Centraid application and the Assist ceremony service at oauth.centraid.dev. It does not replace the policies of Google, Cloudflare, a model provider you configure, or the operator of a gateway you do not control.

§02 · Google user data

Only the capabilities you choose.

At connect time, you choose which supported capability to authorize. Depending on that choice, Centraid may read Google Calendar events, Google Contacts, Gmail messages, or Google Drive file content and metadata; it may send Gmail only when you choose the send capability and approve the corresponding Centraid outbox action. Centraid does not request Google identity scopes such as openid, email, or profile for Assist.

Your gateway uses Google data to provide the connector, search, organization, automation, and assistant features you choose. Imported records and derived results are stored in your vault. Centraid does not sell Google user data, use it for advertising, or use it to train a shared Centraid model.

If you invoke a feature that uses an AI/model service, your gateway may send the selected content needed for that user-facing request to the model provider you configured. That provider's terms and privacy policy apply. Assist itself does not receive imported Google data.

Centraid's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

§03 · OAuth custody

A ceremony edge, not a credential cloud.

Google redirects a short-lived authorization code and random state to the Assist Worker. The Worker returns that code to the initiating Centraid client, and the client delivers it over its existing authenticated transport to your gateway. The gateway alone holds the matching PKCE verifier. The Worker's token exchange attaches a confidential Google client secret and returns Google's token response directly to the gateway.

Access and refresh tokens are stored only on your gateway, encrypted at rest. Tokens never enter a browser URL, page, fragment, deep link, browser storage, Cloudflare KV/D1, or a Centraid-hosted connection database. The token response is processed transiently in Worker memory. Cloudflare processes network traffic as the infrastructure provider; Centraid configures no per-user storage and records only aggregate route/outcome/status counters.

After authorization, your gateway—not the Assist Worker—contacts Google APIs. Transport encryption, short-lived single-use ceremony state, PKCE, client/device binding, a callback receipt, rate limits, and sealed gateway storage protect this flow.

§04 · retention and your choices

Disconnect, revoke, delete.

Connector tokens remain on your gateway until you remove/reconfigure the connection, delete the vault, or Google revokes the grant. You can also revoke Centraid Assist from your Google Account's third-party access controls. Removing a connection stops future access and removes its credential; already imported records remain in your vault until you delete those records or the vault.

Centraid's Assist edge retains no token or connection record to delete. Aggregate operational metrics contain no user identity, authorization code, token, request body, or Google data. Standard Cloudflare security/abuse processing may temporarily handle network metadata under Cloudflare's service terms.

You may use the Advanced path with your own OAuth application instead of Assist. BYO credentials and tokens remain on your gateway and do not use the Assist Worker.
